# Student Debt & Career Outcomes Research (Tableau)
**By:** Aisha Nomani, Sai Han Shan Pha, Ngoc Phuong Tam Mai, Ahmed Alazzawi, and Mohammed Aljumai  

---

## Overview  
This project analyzes the relationship between student loan debt and career outcomes in the United States. Using multiple public datasets, we explore how debt has grown over time and whether higher education provides a strong financial return across majors, institutions, and education levels.

---

## Research Questions  
- How has student loan debt per borrower changed over time?  
- What is the relationship between student debt and earnings after graduation?  
- Which majors offer the strongest wage-to-debt ratios?  
- How does education level impact unemployment rates?  
- How do outcomes differ across institution types (public, private nonprofit, for-profit)?  
- Do popular majors provide stronger financial returns?  
- Which fields have the highest and lowest ROI?  

---

## Data Sources  
- Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)  
- College Scorecard (U.S. Department of Education)  
- U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)  
- IPEDS (National Center for Education Statistics)  

---

## Tools & Technologies  
- Tableau  
- Tableau Prep Builder  

---

## 🔍 Methodology  
- Collected and cleaned datasets from multiple sources  
- Removed missing and inconsistent values  
- Joined datasets using common fields (CIP codes, institution names, degree levels)  
- Built interactive visualizations and dashboards in Tableau to analyze trends and relationships  

---

## Key Insights  
- Student loan debt per borrower has drastically increased over time  
- Higher education levels are linked to lower unemployment and higher earnings  
- ROI varies significantly by major, with technical fields showing stronger returns  
- Institution type impacts outcomes, with public and nonprofit schools generally performing better  
- Popular majors are not always the most financially beneficial
